## New major comments

All in or downstream of the new II.D material unless noted.

1. **Nomenclature contradicts the committed c_ν,ai (l. 78 vs l. 713–715, 794,
   1198).** The nomenclature entry reads "laminar-viscosity scale in the SA
   diffusion term only ($1/12$)", but II.D commits $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}=1/6$
   (l. 713–715), and the Fig. 4 caption (l. 794) and constants list (l. 1198)
   agree on 1/6. The 1/12 is residue of the pre-rework constant set. Change the
   nomenclature to $1/6$ — and grep the repo's figure generators to confirm the
   committed figures (fs_nuHat_rows, model_calibrate, onset_graze) were produced
   at the 1/6 + k=0.71 set, since the constants moved after some figures were
   first made.

2. **The constants-list rationale for c_ν,ai contradicts II.D's actual
   criterion (l. 1198 vs l. 701–719).** The constants list justifies 1/6 as
   "the largest value whose added envelope bend does not exceed the envelope's
   intrinsic curvature" — but II.D states the intrinsic curvature is under 1%
   (l. 677–678) while the bend at 1/6 is 17% (l. 704): by the listed criterion
   1/6 fails by an order of magnitude. II.D's actual stopping rule is different
   and well stated: "the largest coefficient that holds the anchored N=9
   prediction inside the scatter of the $e^N$ correlations themselves
   (~7%)" (l. 716–717). Replace the constants-list clause with that rule
   (e.g. "largest value holding the anchored N=9 prediction inside the $e^N$
   correlations' own scatter"). This looks like a leftover from the earlier
   1/12 rationale.

3. **The calibration march Eq. (eq:transport) omits the onset gate that the
   anchor procedure requires (l. 655–659 vs l. 681–683, 731–738).** As printed,
   the march's source is $a(\hat S g)\,\omega\,\hat\nu$ with $a$ the *ungated*
   rate of eq:rate (the pass-2 major-6 convention). But k is "chosen so that
   the march \eqref{eq:transport} … crosses N=1 at … 338" (l. 733–736), and the
   c=1 failure analysis speaks of the march "with the low branch of the
   threshold removed entirely" (l. 681–682) — both require the gate
   $S(Re_\Omega/Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c}(\hat S g))$ inside the march. A reader
   implementing eq:transport literally gets a threshold-free equation on which
   k has no effect, making the anchor unreproducible. Write the source as
   $a(\hat S g)\,S\!\big(Re_\Omega/Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c}(\hat S g)\big)\,\omega\,\hat\nu$
   (or add "with the onset gate of Eq. (eq:onset) included" to the sentence
   following the equation). Check Fig. 4's caption/mention for the same
   omission (it currently lists only $a_{\max}$ and $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}$).

4. **"The three anchor stations" is residue from the three-anchor predecessor
   (l. 989–990).** The back-reaction quantification in Sec. 2.5 says including
   the destruction floor "moves the three anchor stations by less than 1%" —
   but the reworked model has exactly one anchor (the Blasius N=1 inception;
   the graze constants are "never refit", l. 645–646). State what was actually
   re-marched (presumably the Blasius N=1 and N=9 crossings, or the anchor
   plus checks); as written it contradicts the paper's own one-anchor story
   in the very section that sells it. The nearby phrase "the assembled
   favorable family's mean by −2.6%" (l. 991) is also unclear — see minor 8.

5. **Stale flat-plate agreement number in the receptivity subsection (l.
   1099–1101 vs l. 1294–1298, 2264–2268).** Sec. 2.6 promises Mack's map's
   "flat-plate consequence is quantified in Sec. 3 … (within ~11% of AGS at
   the χ=1 crossing)", but Sec. 3 reports "−1% at Tu=0.04% … 9–17% early over
   the rest of the range (largest, −17%, at Tu=0.30%)" and the Conclusion says
   "within ~17%". The 11% presumably belongs to the pre-rework kernel. Make it
   ~17% (or "up to ~17% early"), and re-verify the Sec. 3 percentages were
   recomputed under the new (C,A,B;k) threshold rather than inherited.

6. **Fig. 3(b) caption disagrees with the text on threshold accuracy (l.
   762–763 vs l. 744–747).** The caption says the onset tracks the
   Drela–Giles stations "within ~±15% across the attached family"; the text
   says "to 5% root-mean-square: the adverse wedges within ±4%, the favorable
   pair 7–10% early". A ±15% bound when the worst quoted member is 10% early
   reads as a different calibration (likely the pre-bb291d8 3-parameter fit's
   number). Quote one consistent statistic in both places — the text's
   rms-plus-worst-case is the more informative.
