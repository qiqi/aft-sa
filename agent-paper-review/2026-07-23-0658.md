# Review of `paper/main.tex` — SA-AI one-equation transition model
*Reviewed 2026-07-23. Line numbers refer to the current `main.tex` (2480 lines).*

## Summary

The paper is in strong shape overall: the central idea (repurposing the sub-O(1) range of the SA working variable as a TS amplification factor) is clearly motivated, the indicator-sphere construction is original and carefully argued, the calibration chain is unusually transparent about what is anchored versus predicted, and the validation matrix (flat plate, NLF(1)-0416, Eppler 387 across two mesh families and three refinement levels, plus a 3D wing) is thorough with honest reporting of weaknesses (favorable-branch under-amplification, over-long bubble). The main remaining problems are internal consistency ones: several claims contradict each other across sections (the N=9 calibrated-vs-predicted story, bubble over-length magnitudes, e^9 cross-code agreement), the Daedalus section leans on undefined "Q1/Q2 gate" terminology left over from an earlier model version, there are two genuine notation collisions (`g`, `P`) inside the assembled-model section itself, a used-but-undefined symbol (λ_p), and a gap between the signed 2D indicator theory and the absolute-valued 3D formulas actually implemented. Fig. 1's PDF is also missing from `figs/` (the build log shows a file-not-found). All of these are fixable without new computations, except possibly the promised final-kernel re-run of the wing.

## Major comments

1. **Undefined "Q1/Q2" gate terminology and unspecified predecessor kernel (Sec. 6, lines 1906–1913; also lines 2056–2079 and Appendix B line 2388).** The Daedalus section states the computations "predate the second gate pinch and carry the predecessor kernel---the $Q_1$-only gate with its own three-anchor constants" and refers to "the in-bubble amplification that $Q_2$ regulates." Neither $Q_1$ nor $Q_2$ nor a "gate pinch" appears anywhere in Sec. 2; the assembled model (Sec. 2.7) has one rate clip and one $Re_\Omega$ onset gate. This is clearly residue from an earlier formulation (cf. `main_pre_q4.tex`). As written, a reader cannot know what model produced the 3D results, making them irreproducible from the paper. Fix: either (a) re-run the wing with the final kernel (already "planned" per line 1914), or (b) define the predecessor kernel concretely (a short appendix paragraph: its rate/onset forms and its three anchor constants) and purge the $Q_1/Q_2$ jargon in favor of language defined in Sec. 2.

2. **The supporting cross-reference for the predecessor-kernel error bound is unsupported (lines 1911–1913, 2072–2073).** The claim that the kernel difference "on two-dimensional sections in this wing's Reynolds range shifts bubble transition by at most $0.02\,c$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:eppval})" points to Sec. 5, but Sec. 5 contains no comparison between kernel versions — it reports only final-kernel results. Either add the kernel-vs-kernel 2D comparison (a small table or a sentence with numbers, stating both kernels were run on the E387 L1/L2 grids) or drop the cross-reference and state the evidence explicitly where the claim is made.

3. **Contradiction: is the $N\!=\!9$ Blasius crossing a prediction or a calibration input?** Sec. 2.4 (lines 638–641) is emphatic: "its $N\!=\!9$ transition point is a \emph{prediction}, not an input: with $a_{\max}$ un-tuned it lands at $Re_\theta\!=\!1121$, matching Drela's 1108." But the Fig. 4 caption (lines 670–672) says "the envelope meets the correlation at $N\!=\!1$ and $N\!=\!9$ \emph{by construction} (the onset conditions of Sec.~2.4)," and the Conclusion (lines 2108–2110) says the threshold "is set by \emph{two} points of the Blasius $e^N$ envelope (the $N\!=\!1$ and $N\!=\!9$ crossings)." These cannot all be true, and the distinction is load-bearing for the paper's "anchored, not tuned" narrative. Decide which statement is correct and make all three sites agree; if only $N\!=\!1$ is imposed, fix the caption and the conclusion.

4. **The $N\!=\!1$ anchor itself is fuzzy (lines 622–623 vs. 638–639).** The threshold's "single free scale is fixed by \emph{one} anchor: the Blasius inception must fall at the Drela--Giles $N\!=\!1$ station ($Re_\theta\!=\!338$)" — yet the verification march crosses $N\!=\!1$ at $Re_\theta\!=\!353$, 4.4% off the anchor. Presumably the anchor is imposed on some other reading (the LST neutral point? the gate midpoint?) and the marched envelope is downstream of it because of the ramp width and diffusion. State operationally what quantity was set equal to 338 when fixing $c_R$, so the 353 is a consistency check rather than an apparent failure to hit one's own anchor.

5. **Notation collision on $g$ inside the assembled model (Sec. 2.7).** $g \equiv Y-X-Z$ is the paper's central Rayleigh coordinate (Eq. \eqref{eq:parabola}, used everywhere as $\hat S g$), but Eq. \eqref{eq:sa_closure} (line 1047) reuses $g = r + c_{w2}(r^6-r)$ for the SA wall-function auxiliary — in the same equation block that restates $g = Y-X-Z$ twelve lines later (line 1065). The nomenclature lists only the Rayleigh $g$. A third $g$ appears as the mesh growth ratio "growth-ratio offset $(g-1)$" (line 1252). Rename the SA auxiliary (e.g., $g_w$, with a note that it is Spalart's $g$) and use a plain word for the mesh growth ratio.

6. **Notation collision on $P$.** $P$ is (i) the blended production (Eq. \eqref{eq:blend}, \eqref{eq:aisa_pd}, and the nomenclature), (ii) the rate coordinate, "the rate $P=\hat S g$" (line 504) and "$P = \hat S\,g$" in the assembled gather (line 1065) — both meanings appear in Sec. 2.7 within a few lines of each other — and (iii) lower-case $p$ is the Orr–Sommerfeld production density (line 488). Drop usage (ii) entirely: the paper already has a perfectly good name for $\hat S g$ ("the amplifying/rate coordinate"), so writing $P = \hat S g$ buys nothing and breaks the production symbol.

7. **Gap between the signed 2D indicator theory and the 3D formulas actually implemented (Sec. 2.2 vs. Sec. 2.7, lines 1061–1067).** Section 2.2 builds the theory on signed scalars $(u, du', \tfrac12 d^2u'')$, argues the state is defined only up to sign (RP²), and derives that "only \emph{even} products … are well defined," with $\hat S$ and $g$ each odd and $\hat S g$ their legitimate even product. The assembled model instead uses $(|\mathbf{u}|,\ \omega d,\ \tfrac12 d^2\,\hat{\mathbf n}\!\cdot\!\nabla\omega)$ with $\omega$ the vorticity *magnitude*: $X$ and $Y$ are then non-negative, $\hat S \ge 0$ is no longer odd, and the projective algebra of Sec. 2.2 no longer literally applies. Three specific things need reconciling: (a) $|\mathbf u|$ is even but not smooth at $u=0$ — exactly the velocity-zero longitude that the separated-profile narrative (lines 475–481) highlights as the physically decisive crossing; state whether the kink is harmless there and why. (b) With $\omega = |{-u'}|$, the wall-normal derivative $\hat{\mathbf n}\!\cdot\!\nabla\omega = \mathrm{sign}(u')\,u''$, so the 3D $Z$ differs in sign from the 2D $\tfrac12 d^2 u''$ wherever the flow is reversed — is $g = Y - X - Z$ then still the Rayleigh coordinate on the reversed branch of Fig. 1? (c) A sentence proving (or asserting with argument) that the implemented magnitude-based $\hat S g$ coincides with the theoretical even product on the physically visited region would close the gap. Right now the "projective algebra" argument (lines 397–404), presented as "not cosmetic," is not obviously satisfied by the implemented formulas.

8. **Contradictory bubble over-length magnitudes (Sec. 5 line 1713 vs. Conclusion lines 2131–2132).** Sec. 5: "a bubble $0.04$–$0.06\,c$ too long" (from reattachment offsets 0.78 vs 0.74, 0.73 vs 0.67, 0.64 vs 0.59). Conclusion: "over-predicting the bubble length by $0.09$–$0.11\,c$ at low incidence." If the conclusion's number includes an earlier separation point (so total length error exceeds reattachment error), say so and give the separation-point numbers in Sec. 5; otherwise make the two ranges agree.

9. **Contradiction on cross-code $e^9$ agreement (Table \ref{tab:eppresweep} footnote, lines 1788–1791, vs. Sec. 4 lines 1448–1453).** The footnote asserts that away from $Re=6\times10^4$ "all three [mfoil, XFOIL, FlexFoil] agree to better than 1% in $c_l$ and $c_d$ and $0.005\,c$ in $x_\mathrm{tr}$" — at "every other condition (both airfoils, all incidences, …)". But Sec. 4 reports mfoil at $0.62\,c$ vs XFOIL at $0.52\,c$ on the NLF $\alpha=0^\circ$ lower front — a $0.10\,c$ spread the text itself analyzes ("about 1.5 units of accumulated amplification"). Qualify the footnote (e.g., exclude the flat-envelope NLF lower front, or restrict the $x_\mathrm{tr}$ claim to the Eppler conditions).

10. **$\lambda_p$ is used but never defined, and collides with $\lambda$ (Sec. 4, lines 1211–1229).** The pressure-gradient parameter $\lambda_p$ appears three times with quantitative values ("band-averaged $\lambda_p$ passes $-1$", "$\lambda_p\sim-0.1$ to $-2$") but is defined nowhere — and $\lambda$ is already taken as the sphere longitude (Eq. \eqref{eq:shat}, nomenclature). Define it (presumably a Pohlhausen-type $(\theta^2/\nu)\,dU_e/dx$ or Drela's $\lambda$?) or replace with a defined quantity; add to the nomenclature; consider renaming to avoid the longitude collision.

11. **The onset gate's functional form arrives 4 subsections after the gate does.** $S(\cdot)$ is introduced at Eq. \eqref{eq:onset} (line 570) with no functional form; the ramp width $w=0.35$ is used at Eq. \eqref{eq:transport} (line 636) still with no form; only Sec. 2.7 (line 1072) reveals $S(\zeta)=\tfrac12[1+\tanh((\zeta-1)/w)]$. Give the tanh form at Eq. \eqref{eq:onset}. Relatedly: the clip notations $\operatorname{clip}\langle\cdot\rangle_0^1$ (Eq. \eqref{eq:rate}) and $\langle\cdot\rangle_+$ (line 1073) are never defined — define both at first use; and the gate symbol $S$ sits uncomfortably close to $\hat S$ and $\tilde S$ (three S's in Eq. \eqref{eq:pai}) — consider $G(\cdot)$ or $F_{on}$.

12. **Three onset constants vs. "single free scale" claim (Sec. 2.4, lines 608–623).** The text says the threshold's "shape follows from linear theory" and its "single free scale is fixed by one anchor," but $(c_R, q, Re_\Omega^{\mathrm f}) = (88, 0.6, 65)$ is three numbers, and the LST collapse that supposedly fixes $q$ and the floor is asserted, never shown. A one-panel figure of the Falkner–Skan neutral-point $Re_\Omega$ data versus $\hat S g$ with the fitted power law overlaid (a candidate already exists as `onset_collapse.png` in the paper directory) would make the "shape from LST, scale from one anchor" claim verifiable and defuse the obvious referee question of how $q=0.6$ and the floor 65 were chosen.

13. **Fig. 1 is missing from the build.** `figs/indicator_sphere.pdf` does not exist (the only copy is a PNG under `repro/analytic/figs_explore/`, and `main.log` records a file-not-found). Per the repo's own onboarding notes, the figure has no committed generator. Since Fig. 1 is the paper's conceptual centerpiece, commit the generator and the PDF before anything else.

14. **Numerics/implementation section is missing.** For a model whose new indicators involve a *second* wall-normal velocity derivative ($\hat{\mathbf n}\!\cdot\!\nabla\omega$) evaluated from least-squares gradients on stretched, sometimes near-degenerate cells (the paper itself stresses this at lines 1306–1311), the reader needs: how $\nabla\omega$ is discretized; how $\hat S = Y/\sqrt{X^2+Y^2}$ is guarded at $d\to0$ where $X=Y=Z=0$; whether the new source is treated implicitly and what its Jacobian contribution is; and something about iterative convergence/cost relative to baseline SA (the paper reports only final force flatness). Half a page would do, and would greatly help implementers — a stated aim of the assembled-model section.

15. **Abstract slightly overstates the minimality and omits the 3D result.** "one modified production term drives the SA transport equation, unchanged in structure" — yet Sec. 2.7 (lines 1023–1025) itself lists *three* modifications (blended production, tied destruction, reduced molecular diffusion coefficient). "Unchanged in structure" is defensible; "one modified production term" is not quite. Also the abstract's "tested on three cases" silently omits the Daedalus wing — the paper's only 3D evidence and arguably its most novel validation; one sentence would earn it readers.

16. **Section 2.5/2.6 ordering forces forward references.** $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}$ is used in the calibration instrument (Eq. \eqref{eq:transport}, Sec. 2.4) and throughout the wall-layer analysis of Sec. 2.5, but is only introduced and motivated in Sec. 2.6 (\ref{sec:nulamscale}). Moving the diffusion-reduction subsection ahead of the onset calibration (or at least ahead of Sec. 2.5) would let the paper read forward. Related: Sec. 2.5 twice cites "Sec.~\ref{sec:calib_diff}" from *inside* Sec. 2.5 (lines 711, 715) — self-references that should read "below"/"at the end of this subsection."

17. **Internal inconsistencies between Sec. 6/Fig. 3 and Appendix B.** (a) The separation-limit shape factor is $H=4.03$ in the Fig. 3 caption (line 595) but $H=3.98$ in Appendix B (line 2440). (b) Sec. 6 says the resolved band keeps the excess "within $\sim30\%$ of the correlation" (line 2070) while Appendix B says frozen separated profiles return "0.9–1.9× the correlation" (line 2460) — a 90% excess at the top of that range. Reconcile the numbers or explain that they measure different things (which profiles, which $H$ range).

18. **Missing comparisons and citations.** (a) The E387 at $Re=2\times10^5$ is chosen *because* AFT, $\gamma$–$Re_\theta$, and BCM all publish it (lines 1527–1531), yet no quantitative comparison against those published results appears anywhere — the conclusion defers even the BCM comparison to future work. Even a small table of literature transition/reattachment locations and $c_d$ at $\alpha=5^\circ$ would let the reader place SA-AI in the field, at nearly zero cost. (b) Tools without citations: AVL (Drela & Youngren), Construct2D; consider also primary references for Falkner–Skan and Stewartson's reversed-flow solutions, which carry real weight in Secs. 2.1–2.4. (c) The flat plate uses a single 320×80 grid with no refinement statement, unlike every other case in the paper — one sentence confirming grid-insensitivity of the onsets would close the loop.

## Minor comments

1. Line 1358–1359: "the sphere rate coordinate coordinate $\hat S g$" — doubled word.
2. Line 1024: "except in three places---the blended production $\hat S g$, …" — the blended production is $P$; $\hat S g$ is the coordinate inside it.
3. Lines 196–199: "Table~\ref{tab:taxonomy} has one empty cell" — the table as printed has no empty cell (the SA-AI row fills it). Reword ("had one empty cell, which the last row fills") or add the visual gap the text promises.
4. Eq. \eqref{eq:scaling}: "$\lesssim O(1)$" mixes an inequality with Landau notation; "$= O(1)$" or "$\lesssim 1$".
5. Nomenclature says $\omega$ is the vorticity *magnitude*, but line 458 uses the signed convention "($\omega=-u'$)". State the 2D sign convention where it is used.
6. Line 488: $\alpha$ in the Orr–Sommerfeld production density $p=\tfrac{\alpha}{2}|\mathrm{Im}(\phi'\phi^*)||U'|$ is the streamwise wavenumber, but $\alpha$ is the paper's angle of attack; note the local meaning (and $p$ vs $P$, cf. major 6).
7. Eq. \eqref{eq:transport} writes $\sigma_\mathrm{SA}$ where the rest of the paper writes $\sigma$; unify.
8. Nomenclature: the entry "$\hat S g$ ⟩ amplifying coordinate $\hat S\,g$ (even on $\mathbb{RP}^2$)" is self-referential; $Re_\Omega^{\mathrm f}$ appears in the tabbing kill-line but has no entry; $w$, $c_R$, $q$, $\sigma_t$, and $\lambda_p$ (if kept) are missing.
9. Constants list line 1090: "$(A,B)=(-8.43, 2.4)$" — $A$ and $B$ never appear symbolically in Eq. \eqref{eq:tumap}; either write $N_\mathrm{crit} = A - B\ln Tu_\mathrm{frac}$ there or drop the names.
10. Line 2044 says the wing $\chi=1$ front is "cyan" while the Figs. 15–17 caption says "bold"; make the color/weight reference consistent with the actual figure.
11. Line 1350: "the fault lies in mfoil's Newton globalization" — a fairly pointed diagnosis of someone else's code; consider softening ("appears to lie in") unless verified.
12. Both `spalart_allmaras_1992` and `spalart_allmaras_1994` are cited for the baseline model (lines 174, 274, 757, 952); if intentional (conference vs. journal versions), fine, but check the \S II.2–II.5 section numbers cited at line 953 match the 1994 version specifically.
13. Line 1128 vs 1494: Somers polar cited as "Fig.~11d" for the drag polar and "Fig.~9d" for the transition orifices — verify both figure numbers against TP-1861.
14. Line 84 nomenclature and line 92: "$|u|$ velocity magnitude relative to the nearest-wall point" — this (Galilean-invariance / moving-wall) subtlety never surfaces in the body text; one sentence at Eq. \eqref{eq:indicators}'s 3D restatement would justify it.
15. Line 1063: $R=\sqrt{X_0^2+Y_0^2+Z_0^2}$ defined via "unnormalized components" named only after use; simpler to write $R = \sqrt{|\mathbf u|^2 + (\omega d)^2 + (\tfrac12 d^2\hat{\mathbf n}\cdot\nabla\omega)^2}$ directly.
16. Line 634: "the same cross-stream diffusion as the coupled model" — but Eq. \eqref{eq:transport} keeps only the molecular part ($c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}$), dropping the $\tilde\nu\nabla^2\tilde\nu$ and $c_{b2}$ terms; the sentence should say so (the justification exists at lines 926–929).
17. Inconsistent citation style for equations: "Eq.~\eqref{…}" vs bare "(\eqref{…})" (e.g., lines 556, 1362); AIAA style prefers "Eq. (n)".
18. Line 1904–1905: the flight $N_\mathrm{crit}\approx13.6$ choice would benefit from a citation (e.g., Drela's Daedalus design notes) since it drives the entire wing comparison.
19. Fig. 2 caption (line 603): "reflecting the one calibrated quantity, the threshold $Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c}(\hat S g)$" — with three constants in Eq. \eqref{eq:reomc}, "quantity" should be "function" (and see major 12).
20. Line 869: "Does the modification change SA's tolerance to coarse near-wall meshes…?" — the rhetorical-question construction is fine once, but consider a declarative heading-style sentence for AIAA journal tone.
