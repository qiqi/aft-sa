## New major comments

1. **The Re=10⁵ "bistable branch" is a still-decaying transient in the
   committed histories, and the section's headline claim is not yet
   established.** `tab:eppfork` + l. 2335–2352. Quartile medians of CL
   over the 20,000-iteration fork runs: structured
   0.967→0.911→0.861→0.823, still falling at **−6.6×10⁻³ per 1000
   iterations** at the end (cold start 0.769, reached in ~7000 more
   iterations at that slope); unstructured 0.962→0.912→0.892→0.878,
   still falling at −1.9×10⁻³/1000 it, and its decile decrements
   (−0.0077, −0.0058, −0.0049, −0.0039, ratio ≈0.78) extrapolate
   geometrically to ≈0.861 — the cold-start value (0.8595) — not to a
   distinct branch. Three textual consequences: (a) "both families
   retain a higher-lift, lower-drag state" describes medians over the
   final 20% of an unconverged decay, not a held state; (b) "the
   unstructured fork's lift lands on the measured value" (0.877 vs
   0.873) is the moving average photographed as it crossed the
   measurement — the final decile is already 0.8753 and falling; (c)
   the asterisk's "slow ΔC≈6×10⁻³ **limit cycle**" is wrong in kind:
   detrending the structured tail leaves ±0.0018 of ripple around a
   −6.6×10⁻³/1000-it secular ramp — the quoted 6×10⁻³ *is* the ramp.
   What the data honestly support: at 6×10⁴ the warm start returns to
   the burst branch within 20k iterations; at 10⁵ it has not — the
   relaxation is an order of magnitude slower and had not terminated
   when the budget ran out. Either extend the forks until they plateau
   or return (the decisive, cheap experiment — the cav fork needs
   perhaps another 20–40k iterations), or rewrite the subsection's
   claim from "the model is bistable near 10⁵" to the demonstrated
   slow-relaxation statement, and fix the caption's asterisk. Note the
   *bursting-boundary*-one-step-high result (cold sweep, refinement
