## Major comments

1. **The march's justification for dropping destruction contradicts
   Sec. II.E's own definition of the blend.** l. 888–891: "no SA
   production or destruction enters---those are switched off where
   $\chi\!\ll\!1$ by the handover weights of
   Sec.~\ref{sec:blend}." Production, yes: $\sigma_t=0$ for $\chi\le1$
   exactly (Eq. eq:sigma). Destruction, no — Sec. II.E states, with
   emphasis, "The destruction is not gated off but \emph{tied}"
   (l. 1037), and the tie $\sigma_D = 1-\tfrac{c_{b1}}{\kappa^2
   c_{w1}}(1-\sigma_P)$ (l. 1028) leaves $\sigma_D\approx0.751\neq0$ at
   $\sigma_P=0$: a destruction floor is present on the laminar branch by
   design, and the same subsection quantifies its residue ("ratio to the
   amplification production stays below $10^{-3}$", l. 1044–1047). The
   previous wording ("those belong to the turbulent side") was vague but
   not false; the new one is crisper and wrong for one of the two terms.
   Fix in one clause, e.g.: "production is switched off outright
   ($\sigma_P\!=\!0$ for $\chi\!\le\!1$) and the tied destruction floor
   is inert at the disturbance's scale ($<10^{-3}$ of the amplification,
   Sec.~\ref{sec:blend})."

2. **Fig-3 caption: "stretching the growth over a run nearly three times
   Blasius's" does not reproduce under any natural metric.** l. 926–928.
   Re-marching both rows at the committed settings: the N=1→N=9 run in
   $Re_\theta$ is 1488 (β=+0.10) vs 844 (Blasius) = **1.76×**; the
   $Re_\theta$ at N=9 is 2359 vs 1192 = **2.0×**; in $Re_x$ the N=1→N=9
   span is **4.5×** and the N=9 station **4.8×** (2.11×10⁶→1.54×10⁷ vs
   2.75×10⁵→3.22×10⁶). Nothing lands near 3 (the commit message's
   "growth stretched ~3x Blasius" repeats the claim). Either state the
   metric that gives ~3× (I could not construct one short of comparing
   the displayed panel domains) or re-quote: "nearly twice Blasius's in
   $Re_\theta$" or "over four times in $Re_x$" both reproduce.
