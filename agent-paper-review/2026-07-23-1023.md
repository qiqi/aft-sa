## New major comments

1. **`figs/model_calibrate.pdf` is stale against its rewritten caption and
   generator.** The caption (l. 791–806, rewritten 10:26) now claims panel (b)
   compares against "the Drela--Giles $N\!=\!1$ station
   $Re_{\theta 0}+1/(dN/dRe_\theta)$" and that "both vertical axes extend
   slightly past the Drela range to show the favorable-regime departure" —
   and its generator `repro/analytic/fig02_model_calibrate.py` (mtime 10:24)
   implements exactly that (`N1_g = Rtc_g + 1.0/drela_g`, line 98). But the
   shipped `figs/model_calibrate.pdf` is from 09:14 — *older than the
   generator edit* — so the compiled paper shows a figure that does not match
   its caption (the old axes-span-Drela-range-only version, whose caption said
   ±15%). Regenerate the PDF, and verify the caption's "~±10%" and the text's
   "5% rms / ±4% / 7–10% early" (l. 758–762) against the regenerated panel.
   For contrast, `fs_nuHat_rows.pdf` (10:25) *was* regenerated after its
   generator (10:24), and the other Sec.-II figures postdate their
   generators. (This is exactly the figure/caption race the pass-3 minor 10
   warned about; per the repo's own build-consistency habits, a
   regenerate-all before each compile would prevent the class of error.)

2. **Appendix B still describes a "sigmoid" rate that exists nowhere in the
   paper (l. 2607–2608, 2620–2621).** "the anchor condition of
   Sec.~\ref{sec:calib_rate} pins the sigmoid's flank at a third of the
   ceiling" and "$1.45\times$ in temporal rate (saturated sigmoid versus
   wall-damped mode)". The current rate (Eq. eq:rate) is a *linear clip*,
   $a=a_{\max}\operatorname{clip}\langle\hat Sg\rangle_0^1$ — no sigmoid —
   and Sec. II.C contains no anchor condition (the single anchor is the
   Blasius N=1 crossing, set in II.E on the threshold scale $k$, not on the
   rate). This is predecessor-kernel language (the old shaped-rate variant)
   surviving in a paragraph whose opening claims to describe "the comparisons
   of Secs.~II.C and~VI". The paragraph does flag lower down that the ledger
   was "measured on the predecessor kernel these wing computations carry"
   (l. 2623–2624), but the anchor-condition sentence and the
   "$0.35\,a_{\max}$ flank" interpretation read as statements about the
   current model and are false of it. Fix jointly with P1-1: either restate
   the ledger in current-kernel terms (the linear rate at the
   separation-limit $\hat S g$ is directly computable) or explicitly attribute
   the sigmoid and its anchor to the predecessor kernel *when it is defined*.

3. **Two cross-references point at `sec:calib_rate` for content that moved to
   II.E in the restructure.** (a) l. 1003–1004: "The calibration instrument
   of Sec.~\ref{sec:calib_rate} omits the term" — the instrument
   (Eq. eq:transport) now lives in II.E (`sec:nulamscale`/`sec:calib_onset`);
   II.C contains no march. (b) l. 1877: "the separation-edge softening seen

   Fig.~\ref{fig:calibrate})" — Fig. `model_calibrate` and the softening
   discussion (l. 810–825) are in II.E; II.C carries only the one-line teaser
   "softening only at the separation edge (below)" (l. 537–538). The
   remaining `\S\ref{sec:calib_rate}` citations (l. 1734, 1965, 1984, 2299)
   attribute the over-long bubble to the *linear rate*, which II.C does
   define, so they are defensible — but a one-pass sweep deciding each
   pointer (rate → II.C; march/threshold/softening/figure → II.E) would cost
