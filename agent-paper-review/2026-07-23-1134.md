## New major comments

1. **The validation results of Secs. III–V were computed with the previous
   kernel, and the paper does not say so.** The whole-threshold change landed
   at 10:53 today (d71d85f: `saai_env.py` ceiling 2600 → 1851.2, A
   123.9 → 124.6, B 1.416 → 1.424); every coupled-RANS figure and table in
   Secs. III–V (flat plate, NLF, Eppler, Reynolds sweep — 53+ solutions)
   necessarily predates it and carries the low-branch-k canon, as the 1050
   response itself concedes ("[the flat-plate] values will be refreshed from
   the re-run campaign at the new whole-equation canon"). The low branch moved
   only +0.6%, so the adverse/bubble results (Eppler, high-α NLF) are almost
   certainly insensitive — but the **ceiling moved −28.8%**, and the ceiling
   binds exactly where Sec. IV's central claim lives: the strongly favorable
   NLF rooftop held laminar at $Re_\theta$ far above critical, and the
   low-$Tu$ flat-plate onsets. Unlike Sec. VI, which candidly discloses its
   predecessor kernel, Secs. III–V present these results as if produced by the
   Sec.-II model. Fix (in order of preference): (a) re-run the campaign at
   the committed canon; (b) re-run one exposed sentinel case (NLF α=0° or 4°,
   plus the lowest-$Tu$ plate) and either state the shift is below the quoted
   precision or refresh; (c) at minimum, a dated-canon disclosure paragraph
   like Sec. VI's. Until one of these happens the paper's model definition and
   its evidence are different kernels.

2. **Two favorable-branch onset numbers in II.E escaped the whole-threshold
   update (l. 819–824).** "At $\beta\!\approx\!0.35$ … the onset
   $\approx\!3\times$ its critical $Re_\theta$; by $\beta\!\approx\!0.55$
   transition has retreated beyond any station of practical relevance, and
   stronger favorable wedges are suppressed outright." These sentences are
   unchanged in the d71d85f diff, i.e. they were measured when the ceiling
   kept its inviscid value 2600; with the ceiling now ×0.712 the favorable
   onset drops roughly in proportion, so "≈3×" and the 0.55 boundary are
   presumptively stale (the adjacent family statistics — 6% rms, 11–12%
   early — *were* updated in the same paragraph block, which makes the
   omission conspicuous). The rate number ("≈0.1× Drela") is k-free and
   stands. Re-read both numbers off the regenerated `fig04` marcher and
   update.

3. **Graze-quality scope mismatch between body and Fig. 2 caption
   (l. 649–651 vs l. 613–616).** The body: the graze "fixes the three shape
   constants to a root-mean-square graze error of $\sim\!6\%$ over the
   attached family". The caption: ratios 0.93–1.14 (rms ~6%) "from incipient
   separation through $\beta\!=\!+0.15$, rising to $\sim\!1.3$ at the strongly
   favorable $\beta\!=\!+0.35$ edge". A member 30% off the envelope is not
   inside a 6% rms — the body's "over the attached family" silently excludes
   the family's most favorable member, and it is the sentence the "never
   refit" claim leans on. Carry the caption's qualification into the body
   (e.g. "to ~6% rms from incipient separation through $\beta=+0.15$; the
   $\beta=+0.35$ edge grazes ~30% high, consistent with the favorable-branch
   departure of Fig. 4b").

4. **The c_ν,ai-sweep sequence and the destruction-floor back-reaction were
   not re-derived at the whole-equation anchor.** The sweep percentages
   (bends 24→21→18→17→14%; N=9 +18/+11/+7/+2, l. 749–755, "each candidate
   re-anchored as below") and the tie's back-reaction numbers (<1% anchor
   shift, −2.6% favorable-family mean, l. 1007–1010) come from generators
   last touched before the whole-equation change (`explore_cnuai_sweep.py`
   07:20 vs the 10:24–10:53 rework); only the committed endpoint (+7% at
   1/6, 1181) is verified at the new canon. Since the low branch barely moved,
   the numbers likely survive to the quoted precision — but the favorable-family
   −2.6% shares major 2's exposure (favorable stations moved 7–10%→11–12%
   early). One sweep re-run closes both; alternatively state which anchor
   variant produced them.
