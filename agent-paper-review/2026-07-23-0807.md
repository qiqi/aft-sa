## Summary

The draft has improved substantially since pass 1, and in fact more than the response letter claims: beyond the 11 majors and 16 minors marked ADDRESSED, three items the response deferred are now resolved in the current file — the missing Fig. 1 (`figs/indicator_sphere.pdf` is present and the build log is clean), the N=1 anchor's operational definition (the 338-vs-353 discrepancy is gone), and the onset-threshold verifiability figure (a new Fig. 2, `onset_graze.pdf`, showing the Falkner–Skan graze construction). The onset threshold itself has been reformulated as the soft-min form `Re_Ω^c(Ŝg) = k·softmin₂(C, A + B(Ŝg)⁻²)` with constants (C,A,B;k)=(2600,175,2;0.64), and the text, nomenclature, and constants table are consistent with the new form. The notation collisions (g, P), the λ_p definition, the clip/gate definitions, the bubble-length and e⁹-footnote contradictions, and the missing citations are all verifiably fixed.

The largest remaining problems are (a) the still-open pass-1 items around the Daedalus predecessor kernel (Q1/Q2 jargon, unsupported 0.02c cross-reference) and the signed-2D vs. magnitude-3D gap, all acknowledged and deferred; and (b) a cluster of *new* internal contradictions, the worst of which is that the abstract claims the amplification rate depends on "a pressure-gradient parameter" — the exact opposite of the paper's central selling point, stated in at least five places in the body ("the model itself contains no pressure-gradient parameter"). A matching piece of stale residue survives in Sec. 4 ("the factor is inert"). There are also two fresh number inconsistencies introduced or exposed by the revision (N=9 agreement 1% vs 2%; Eppler L2 reattachment family-spread 0.005c vs the table's 0.016–0.018c), and the curvature indicator is now written three different ways in three sections.

## New major comments

1. **The abstract contradicts the model on the pressure-gradient parameter (l. 44–47).** "The amplification rate depends only on the local mean flow---a vorticity Reynolds number, a profile-fullness indicator, a signed profile-curvature indicator, and a pressure-gradient parameter". The body states, emphatically and repeatedly, that the model *contains no pressure-gradient parameter* (l. 601–602, 1298–1299, 1562–1568, 2207–2209, 2245–2246) — it is the paper's central selling point for the favorable rooftop. This is residue from an earlier variant. Also, "profile-fullness indicator" and "signed profile-curvature indicator" never appear in the body's vocabulary. Rewrite the list in the paper's own terms, e.g., "a vorticity Reynolds number and two profile-shape indicators — a shear fraction and an inflectional (Rayleigh) coordinate — with no pressure-gradient parameter".

2. **Same stale residue in Sec. 4 (l. 1562–1570), plus self-duplication.** The paragraph says "no pressure-gradient factor acts anywhere on the airfoil. On the adverse-gradient Eppler cases of Sec.~5 the factor is inert and the results are unchanged to roundoff." If no factor exists, nothing can be "inert", and "unchanged to roundoff" relative to *what*? This is clearly a leftover from a variant that carried an explicit pressure-gradient factor. Delete the last sentence (or restate what comparison it reports). The same paragraph also says the same thing twice ("holds the favorable rooftop laminar with no pressure-gradient term, setting the fronts where the recovery turns the flow inflectional" ≈ "Over the strongly accelerated rooftop Ŝg→0 forbids growth outright; amplification is confined to the recovery stretch…") — keep one.

3. **Conclusion's Eppler reattachment agreement number contradicts the table (l. 2239–2240 vs Table 8).** "the two independently-generated mesh families agree … to $0.005\,c$ on the Eppler reattachment at L2" — but tab:eppxtr's L2 columns give family spreads of 0.018c (α=0°: 0.760 vs 0.778), 0.016c (2°), 0.016c (5°), 0.002c (7°); and Sec. 5's own text says "within $0.02\,c$ at L2" (l. 1804–1805). Only the α=7° case supports 0.005c. Change to 0.02c (or "0.002–0.018c").

4. **N=9 agreement is ~2% in one place and ~1% in two (l. 686–687 vs l. 719 and l. 2207).** Sec. 2.4 now reads "lands at $Re_\theta\!=\!1131$, matching Drela's 1108 to $\sim\!2\%$" (1131/1108 = +2.1%, correct), while the Fig. 4 caption says "agreeing with Drela's to $\sim\!1\%$" and the Conclusion "reproduced to $\sim\!1\%$". (Pass 1 had 1121, i.e. ~1%; the value evidently moved to 1131 with the soft-min kernel and only one of the three sites was updated.) Make all three say ~2% — or verify which number belongs to the current kernel.

5. **The curvature indicator is defined three different ways (Sec. 2.7 l. 1113, Sec. 3 l. 1173–1178, Sec. 4 l. 1398–1400).** The assembled model — the implementer's reference — defines $Z \propto \hat{\mathbf n}\!\cdot\!\nabla\omega$; the implementation paragraph says the solver evaluates $u''=(\nabla^2\mathbf{u})\cdot\hat{\mathbf{u}}$ via a compact Laplacian (explicitly *because* least-squares gradients of $\omega$ — i.e., the Sec. 2.7 formula read literally — admit odd–even noise); Sec. 4 writes the curvature term as "$\tfrac12 d^2\nabla^2 u$". These agree only for a parallel layer and only up to sign conventions. State once, in Sec. 2.7, the normative continuous definition *and* that the implementation evaluates it as $(\nabla^2\mathbf{u})\cdot\hat{\mathbf u}$ (exact for a parallel layer), then use one notation everywhere. This is separable from (and cheaper than) the deferred pass-1 major 7, but a fix here should be coordinated with it.

6. **The symbol $a$ has two inequivalent definitions (Eq. (eq:rate) l. 529–532 vs assembled model l. 1123–1124).** Section 2.3 defines $a = a_{\max}\operatorname{clip}\langle\hat S g\rangle_0^1$ (no gate; the onset gate is introduced as a separate multiplicative factor in eq:pai), while Sec. 2.7 defines $a = a_{\max}\operatorname{clip}\langle\hat S g\rangle\,S(Re_\Omega/Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c})$ (gate included). Both feed $P_\mathrm{AI}=a\,\omega\tilde\nu$. A reader checking eq:pai_def against the assembled model gets two different sources. Give the gated quantity its own name in Sec. 2.7 (or fold the gate into $a$ from eq:pai onward and say so).
