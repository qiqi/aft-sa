sound where it is fresh: II.C now settles the rate's accuracy *before* any
transport equation, via the frozen-profile spatial eigenproblem
(Eq. eq:frozeneig), and every number in the new Tables 2 and 3 and the

and the receptivity map is cleanly re-homed to Sec. III with a correct teaser
from the assembled-model constants list. Separately, the uncommitted 14:30
edits quietly clear most of pass 5's punch list: the graze-scope mismatch
(P5-M3), the favorable-boundary numbers (β 0.55→0.45 with a concrete marched
station), k quoted as 0.712 throughout, the `a·y` sublayer collision, the
Fig.-2 "at k=1" qualifier, and an essentially complete American-spelling sweep.

Two clusters dominate what remains. First, pass 5's headline finding is still
open and now *visibly in flight*: the Eppler campaign has been re-run at the
live whole-equation canon (the results JSON committed in ed01426 differs
materially from what the paper prints — cavity-L2 α=5° drag +14%, L2 family

surrounding text and tables still describe the old L1-only, old-canon study —
while Sec. II's l. 1322 claims the committed constants were used for "every
coupled-RANS computation." NLF and flat-plate campaigns are not re-run.
Second, the restructure left a small trail of stale pointers where material
moved: Appendix B attributes its packet-speed temporal-units conversion to a
Sec.-II comparison that now advertises "no wave-speed convention on either
side," the Daedalus section cites "the map of Sec. II" for a receptivity map
that now lives in Sec. III, and the new Table 2 (H=3.98 at incipient
separation, script-verified) makes the fig:calibrate caption's H=4.03
definitively the stale number. The Daedalus predecessor-kernel debt (P1-1/2)
is unchanged and now three model revisions old.
