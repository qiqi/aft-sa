## Minor comments

1. **Notation split for the same quantity: $Re_{\theta 0}(H)$ vs
   $Re_{\theta c}(H)$.** The new text (l. 640, 701, 715, 737, 750, and
   pre-existing l. 945) writes the Drela–Giles critical Reynolds number as
   $Re_{\theta 0}(H)$, while the onset-graze figure caption and body
   (l. 786, 798, 808) call the *same* quantity — verbatim "its Drela--Giles
   critical Reynolds number" — $Re_{\theta c}(H)$. Before 1419825 the split
   existed at one buried site (old l. 942 vs 783); the masking commit made
   $Re_{\theta 0}$ the six-fold-repeated, table-column symbol, so a reader
   now meets two symbols for one number within one section. Unify (Drela's
   own notation is $Re_{\theta_0}$), and consider a nomenclature row —
   neither symbol is in the front-matter list (l. 75–100) despite now
   heading a table column.

2. **"$0.64$–$0.85$ even on the separation branch" (l. 646–648) silently
   excludes the deep-separated row.** The sentence opens "the equation
   declines the correlation everywhere the family grows", but the quoted
   range covers only the separation-limit and shallow rows; the
   deep-separated row ($Re_{\theta 0}\!=\!13$, unstable at all four
   stations) reads 0.34–0.41 at $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}\!=\!1$ and 0.41–0.44 at
   $1/6$. The same convention was already embedded in the pre-existing
   "0.82–0.91" claim (pass 7 read the separation branch as the two
   unstarred+shallow rows), so this is consistency, not error — but e9bff0f
   also deleted the only text that explained *why* the deep row sits at half
   (the "left physics" aside on Table 2's 0.46, author-directed). Net
   effect: Table 2's 0.46 and Table 3's 0.34–0.44 now stand with no comment
   anywhere. Not re-litigating the deletion; suggest only a two-word
   qualifier ("on the separation limit and shallow reversed-flow rows") so
   "everywhere the family grows" is not falsified by the table one caption
   below it.

3. **Constants list, l. 1326:** $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}$ is justified as "the
   largest coefficient carrying the frozen-profile growth balance onto the
   $e^N$ correlation *across the transitional decade*". After 1419825 the
   selection story in II.C is sharper and different: the coefficient is
   chosen where the *just-past-critical stations* sit on the correlation,
   with an acknowledged 21% drift by $Re_\theta\!=\!1600$. "across the
   transitional decade" now mildly overstates; echo the new criterion
   ("…onto the $e^N$ correlation at the just-past-critical stations
   (Sec.~II.C, Table~3)").

4. **Stale provenance string in the regenerated generator.**
   `repro/analytic/tab_frozen_slope.py` prints "unstable-station means
   (text quotes Blasius 0.70/0.91/1.00/1.12/1.20)" — the paper no longer
   quotes those means anywhere (the decade-mean sentence was replaced by the
   $Re_\theta\!=\!400$ ladder in this same commit). The ladder half of the
   printout ("text quotes 0.49/0.75/0.87/1.02/1.13") is accurate. One-line
   docstring/print fix so the generator's "text quotes" claims stay true.

5. Wording nit, l. 664–665: "the station just past the Blasius critical
   ($Re_\theta\!=\!400$)" — "critical" used nominally with the noun elided;
   the parallel sentence at l. 645 says "just past the Blasius critical
   *station*". Add the noun (or "its critical station") for the ear.

6. Record only (author-directed, not contested): with the high-Re caveat
   paragraph deleted, the paper no longer discloses anywhere the ~25–30%
   early $N$-accumulation an envelope run completing at
   $Re_\theta\!\gtrsim\!2000$ would see; the only residue is the qualitative
   "climbing toward the finite ceiling of Table 2" clause (l. 684–685). If a
   referee raises cruise-Reynolds low-disturbance applicability, that
