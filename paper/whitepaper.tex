\section{Model}

\subsection{The working variable and its laminar range}

The Spalart--Allmaras (SA) model solves one transport equation for a
working variable $\tilde\nu$, with eddy viscosity
$\mu_t=\rho\,\tilde\nu f_{v1}(\chi)$, $\chi=\tilde\nu/\nu$. The
eddy-viscosity function $f_{v1}(\chi)=\chi^3/(\chi^3+c_{v1}^3)$ vanishes
cubically for $\chi\ll c_{v1}$ (with $c_{v1}=7.1$,
$f_{v1}\approx2.8\times10^{-3}\chi^3$): even at $\chi=1$ the eddy
viscosity is $\sim0.3\%$ of $\rho\tilde\nu$, so the field contributes
negligibly to the mean flow while $\chi\lesssim1$. This sub-$O(1)$ range
is dynamically inert in the baseline model; SA-AI repurposes it: in the
laminar range $\ln(\tilde\nu/\tilde\nu_\infty)$ accumulates as an
$e^N$-style envelope amplification factor, and the working variable
gives rise to its own transition. The equation remains one
(negative-$\tilde\nu$ 2012 safeguards retained):
\begin{equation}
  \frac{D\tilde\nu}{Dt} = P - D
  + \frac{1}{\sigma}\nabla\!\cdot\!\big[(c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}\,\nu+\tilde\nu)\,\nabla\tilde\nu\big]
  + \frac{c_{b2}}{\sigma}\,|\nabla\tilde\nu|^2 ,
  \label{w:sa}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  P = \max\!\big[\,(1-\sigma_P)\,a\,\omega\,\tilde\nu,\;\sigma_P\,P_\mathrm{SA}\,\big],
  \qquad D = \sigma_D\,D_\mathrm{SA},
  \label{w:blend}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_P = \sigma_t(\chi;\tau),\quad
  \sigma_D = 1-\frac{c_{b1}}{\kappa^2c_{w1}}\,(1-\sigma_P),
  \qquad
  \sigma_t(\chi;\tau) = \max\!\big[1-e^{-(\chi-1)/\tau},\,0\big].
  \label{w:sigma}
\end{equation}
$P_\mathrm{SA}=c_{b1}\tilde S\tilde\nu$,
$D_\mathrm{SA}=c_{w1}f_w(\tilde\nu/d)^2$, and all closure functions are
baseline SA. $\sigma_P\!\equiv\!1$ (and with it $\sigma_D\!=\!1$),
$c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}\!=\!1$ recovers baseline SA exactly.

In the laminar range ($\chi\le1$, where $\sigma_P=0$) the production is
the laminar amplification $a\,\omega\,\tilde\nu$, of the same algebraic
form as $P_\mathrm{SA}$: the vorticity magnitude $\omega$ (units
$T^{-1}$) supplies the inverse time scale in place of $c_{b1}\tilde S$,
and $a$ is a nondimensional amplification rate built from local flow
indicators. The physics makes six demands, and the model answers each
in turn. A ceiling $a_{\max}$ pins the rate to its fastest natural
limit, the Kelvin--Helmholtz growth of a detached free shear layer. A
profile-fullness sigmoid throttles that ceiling down to the gentle
Tollmien--Schlichting rate of an attached Blasius layer. A gate $Q$
keeps amplification off the wall---its main job is to be pinched shut
on the attached-wall locus $\omega d=|\mathbf u|$---while opening
across the layer's unstable band. An onset threshold in
$Re_\Omega$---a floor, raised exponentially by favorable pressure
gradient, which also lowers the rate above the threshold---withholds
amplification until the layer can sustain it. A laminar$\to$turbulent
handover returns the equation to standard SA: smoothly, with SA's wall
destruction inert at the seed's quadratic scale, with no
pressure-sensitive residue past the crossover, and with the linear
$\tilde\nu$ wall solution---the log law included---preserved exactly. And a reduced laminar diffusion lets the sub-$O(1)$
disturbance survive its own cross-stream smearing. One more, empirical,
closure---the freestream seed $\chi_\infty$ that sets where the count
begins---closes this section.

\subsection{Local indicators}

The envelope $e^N$ rate $dN/dRe_\theta$ is written in terms of the
momentum thickness $\theta$, which is impractical in a general RANS
field: it requires integration ``to the edge'' of a boundary layer
whose edge may be ill-defined under shear-layer merging, separation, or
freestream turbulence. A practical local surrogate is the vorticity
Reynolds number $Re_\Omega=d^2\omega/\nu$ (van Driest--Blumer, familiar
from the Menter--Langtry framework): in a Blasius layer the peak of
$Re_\Omega$ inside the profile is proportional to $Re_\theta$
($\max_y Re_\Omega\approx2.2\,Re_\theta$) and sits at a fixed multiple
of $\theta$ ($y\approx4.4\,\theta\approx0.6\,\delta_{99}$, near
mid-layer), so $Re_\Omega$ inherits the role of $Re_\theta$ without the
nonlocal integration. It is paired with three local descriptors of the
boundary-layer state, each algebraic in quantities a RANS solver
already carries at a node ($d$ = wall distance, $\omega$ = vorticity
magnitude, $|\mathbf u|$ = speed relative to the nearest wall point):
\begin{equation}
  Re_\Omega = \frac{d^2\omega}{\nu}, \qquad
  \lambda_p = -\frac{d^{2}}{\rho\,\nu\,|\mathbf{u}|^{2}}\frac{Dp}{Dt}, \qquad
  \Gamma = \frac{2(\omega d)^2}{|\mathbf{u}|^2+(\omega d)^2}, \qquad
  \Gamma_g = \frac{\big(d^{2}|\nabla^2\mathbf{u}|\big)^2}{|\mathbf{u}|^2+(\omega d)^2},
  \label{w:ind}
\end{equation}
where $\nabla^2\mathbf u$ is the vector Laplacian of the velocity. All
four are Galilean invariant: the numerator of $\lambda_p$ is the
material derivative $Dp/Dt$ (equal to $\mathbf u\cdot\nabla p$ in the
steady wall frame), the velocity scale $|\mathbf u|$ is taken relative
to the nearest wall point, and $d$, $\omega$, $\nabla p$,
$\nabla^2\mathbf u$ are invariant already. $\lambda_p$ is a local
pressure-gradient parameter of the Pohlhausen/Thwaites type---the same
quantity that enters the Abu-Ghannam \& Shaw correlation through the
Thwaites parameter: positive in a favorable gradient (accelerating
flow), zero on a Blasius profile, negative in an adverse gradient.

$\Gamma\in[0,2]$ is a pointwise measure of velocity-profile fullness:
it approaches unity in an attached near-wall shear layer (where
$\omega d\sim|\mathbf u|$), grows toward $2$ on an inflectional,
near-separation profile, and---whatever the layer's shape---falls to
zero outside it, where the vorticity dies. It contains no integral and
no memory---it reads the profile shape the upstream history has already
produced---so it plays locally the role of the shape factor $H$ in
envelope methods, without being $H$ itself, at the cost of one algebraic
expression per node.
Fig.~1 shows how the Falkner--Skan family populates the
$(Re_\Omega,\Gamma)$ plane: adverse profiles arch over $\Gamma=1$ into
the inflectional range, favorable ones stay attached below it. Past the
separation limit the same equation carries Stewartson's reverse-flow
branch, the canonical analytic separated profile, and it reaches
$\Gamma=2$ exactly at its interior stagnation point ($|\mathbf u|=0$,
$\omega\neq0$): the detached limit that anchors $a_{\max}$ is populated
by an exact solution of the family.

What the instability responds to is the velocity profile, not the
pressure gradient that shaped it: the stability problem is posed on the
profile alone, and both the critical Reynolds number and the $e^9$
transition Reynolds number collapse onto a single shape-factor map
whether the shape is set by pressure gradient, wall heating, or suction
(Wazzan--Gazley--Smith)---the parameterization the Drela--Giles
correlations adopt. (Treating a non-similar profile through the similar
profile of the same $H$ is the working approximation of the envelope
tradition itself, which the model inherits.) The model therefore reads
shape wherever shape can carry the signal, and the division falls along
the sign of the gradient. On the adverse side the shape indicators
carry the onset themselves: at fixed $Re_\theta$ a fuller (higher-$H$)
profile holds a larger $Re_\Omega$---the noses of Fig.~1(a) march from
$2.2\,Re_\theta$ at Blasius to $4.4\,Re_\theta$ at the separation
limit---so it reaches any fixed threshold at a lower $Re_\theta$. And
adverse layers remember: the local gradient can read small downstream
of an adverse run that has since relaxed, while the profile is still
inflectional, so a gradient parameter would misread exactly the layers
that matter most. On the favorable side the situation inverts twice.
The shape family saturates---$H$ compresses from $2.59$ to
$\approx2.2$ across the entire favorable range while the critical
Reynolds number rises by more than an order of magnitude---so no
shape-reading indicator can carry the delay. But the local gradient
becomes trustworthy there: an accelerated laminar layer relaxes toward
the equilibrium profile of its local gradient---for accelerated
power-law edge velocities the downstream convergence to the
Falkner--Skan profile is a theorem, with a rate (Serrin)---and it is
the regime where one-parameter descriptions of the Thwaites type are
most accurate. $\lambda_p$ is thus a faithful shape surrogate precisely
where the shape itself is uninformative; it carries the favorable side
alone---the cliff and rate factor of Eq.~\eqref{w:gate}---and is
clipped to zero on the adverse side, where the shape must be, and is,
read directly.

$\Gamma_g$ reads the curvature of the velocity profile. On a parallel
shear layer
$\nabla^2\mathbf u=(\partial^2u/\partial y^2)\,\hat{\mathbf x}
=(\partial\omega/\partial y)\,\hat{\mathbf x}$---the quantity whose
interior zero defines the inflection point of the Rayleigh
criterion---and in incompressible flow
$\nabla^2\mathbf u=-\nabla\times\boldsymbol\omega$ generally.
$d^2|\nabla^2\mathbf u|$ is a velocity---the same construction that
makes $\omega d$ one---and $\Gamma_g$ compares it, squared, against the
profile's own velocity scale, the denominator it shares with $\Gamma$.
Where the profile is curved---the outer part of any layer, and the
whole neighborhood of an inflection point---$\Gamma_g$ is at least
$O(1)$: across the band it reaches $1.6$ on Blasius, $6$ at the
separation limit, and $O(10)$ on the separated profile of Fig.~1(b); on
the linear near-wall profile it vanishes to high order. It lets the
model tell the layer's unstable band apart from its wall; its only
entry into the model is the gate $Q$, and evaluating it re-uses the
operator the viscous flux already discretizes.

\subsection{The amplification rate}

The four indicators combine into the single nondimensional rate
($a\equiv0$ for $Re_\Omega\le Re_\Omega^{\mathrm{c}}$):
\begin{gather}
  a = a_{\max}\,Q\,S(z)\,f_\lambda, \qquad
  z = s(\Gamma-g_c) + \ln\!\Big(1-\big(Re_\Omega^{\mathrm{c}}(\lambda_p)/Re_\Omega\big)^{p}\Big),
  \qquad S(z)=\frac{1}{1+e^{-z}},
  \label{w:rate}\\
  Q = 1-\frac{\sqrt{\Gamma(2-\Gamma)}}{1 + c_A\,\Gamma_g}, \qquad
  Re_\Omega^{\mathrm{c}}(\lambda_p) = Re_\Omega^{\mathrm{f}}\,
        e^{K_\lambda \max(0,\lambda_p)}, \qquad
  f_\lambda = \frac{1}{1+\big(K_r \max(0,\lambda_p)\big)^2}.
  \label{w:gate}
\end{gather}

\textbf{The ceiling $a_{\max}$} is the one global constant: the fastest
natural amplification, that of a detached free shear layer, fixed as an
eigenvalue (\S3.1). The remaining factors decide where, and how much
of, that ceiling is realized.

\textbf{The sigmoid--barrier switch $S(z)$} superposes two physically
distinct mechanisms. The $\Gamma$ term $s(\Gamma-g_c)$ switches
amplification on as the profile fills out toward the inflectional value
$\Gamma\to2$; its center $g_c$ lies just above the attached asymptote
$\Gamma=1$, so the two ends of the range pin the two physical regimes:
at $\Gamma=2$ the sigmoid is fully on and $a\to a_{\max}$, the growth
rate of a detached shear layer, while attached profiles sit on its
tail, at the small rate of Tollmien--Schlichting growth ($s$ and $g_c$
are fixed in \S3.3). The log-barrier term is an onset threshold: it
drives $z\to-\infty$ as $Re_\Omega\to Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c}$ from
above, so amplification is forbidden until the layer is thick enough,
the residual attenuation fading as
$(Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c}/Re_\Omega)^p$ thereafter. One consequence
follows with no added machinery: since $Re_\Omega=d^2\omega/\nu\to0$ at
the wall, the same threshold suppresses the viscous sublayer
automatically---there $\Gamma\to1$ is a generic near-wall limit, not a
true instability.

\textbf{The favorable-gradient cliff and rate factor $f_\lambda$.} On
the favorable side the shape indicators saturate, so the stabilization
can enter only through the local gradient (\S1.2), and it does so in
two ways: the threshold rises
exponentially with the favorable gradient, and the rate above the
threshold is lowered algebraically. For $\lambda_p\le0$ the threshold
sits exactly at the floor and $f_\lambda=1$, with zero derivative at
$\lambda_p=0$, so Blasius and adverse profiles are untouched to first
order in the gradient. The $|\mathbf u|^2$ denominator of $\lambda_p$
vanishes at stagnation and flow-reversal points; this is harmless
either way: a favorable-sign $\lambda_p\to+\infty$ sends
$Re_\Omega^{\mathrm c}\to\infty$ and $f_\lambda\to0$, switching the
local rate off (the correct no-amplification limit), while an
adverse-sign $\lambda_p$ is clipped by the $\max(0,\lambda_p)$, and
nothing in the rate divides by $\lambda_p$. The division of the
favorable effect---most of the stabilization carried by the onset
delay, a milder correction by the rate---mirrors the $e^N$ correlation:
the critical Reynolds number rises by over an order of magnitude across
the favorable range while the envelope growth rate falls by a factor of
about two. $K_\lambda$ is anchored to the $e^N$ critical Reynolds
number and $K_r$ is fixed by one point of the growth-rate map (\S3.4).
Fig.~2 maps the assembled kernel.

\textbf{The band gate $Q$} exists because amplification must be held
off the wall of an attached layer; away from the wall there is nothing
for it to do, so it should stand as close to fully open, $Q=1$, as
possible. The fraction
subtracted from $1$ has a numerator no larger than $1$, largest exactly
on the attached-wall locus $\Gamma=1$ ($\omega d=|\mathbf u|$), and a
denominator no smaller than $1$, so $0\le Q\le1$, with the gate most
nearly shut where the profile looks like an attached wall and carries
no curvature. There $Q$ collapses to $c_A\Gamma_g/(1+c_A\Gamma_g)$, and
on the linear near-wall profile $\Gamma_g$ vanishes to high order, so
$Q\to0$ at the wall of an attached layer---as the sixth power of wall
distance on a zero-pressure-gradient profile. The pinch keeps the
envelope scale-similar: without it, the thickening layer clears the
cliff ever nearer the wall, amplification creeps inboard, and the
envelope slope $dN/dRe_\theta$ drifts upward without bound instead of
settling to a constant. In the opposite limit $\Gamma\to2$---an
inflectional free-shear profile, or wherever the flow reverses---the
numerator vanishes and $Q\to1$ exactly, so the detached shear layer
feels the full ceiling: the gate does not dilute the free-shear anchor.
Between the limits, the weight $c_A$ (\S3.2) sets how quickly curvature
re-opens the gate toward its ideal; the residual throttling of attached
mid-bands is a side effect the calibration must respect, not an
aim. Jointly with the sigmoid, the gate also sets where a profile
operates: with the wall shut, the gated Blasius profile amplifies most
strongly at $\Gamma\approx0.87$---on the sigmoid's tail, far below the
ceiling, the slowness of Tollmien--Schlichting growth relative to
Kelvin--Helmholtz---while the separation-limit profile operates at
$\Gamma\approx1.6$, on the saturated shoulder.

Gate, sigmoid, barrier, and rate factor all read only the local mean
flow, never $\tilde\nu$, so the amplification rate adds no
$\tilde\nu$-dependence to the source linearization. The production
grows $\tilde\nu$ along the flow at the local rate $a\,\omega$, so
$\ln(\tilde\nu/\tilde\nu_\infty)$ accumulates monotonically in the
manner of an amplification factor $N$; because $a_{\max}$ is small the
accumulation is gentle, on the scale of the $e^N$ envelope (Figs.~3
and~4 quantify the tracking). Transition is reached when $\tilde\nu$
has grown to $\chi\sim1$, where the eddy viscosity becomes dynamically
active.

\subsection{One-equation blend}

The handover weight $\sigma_t$ of Eq.~\eqref{w:sigma} ties the laminar
and turbulent regimes to the single state variable through $\chi$. The
$\max$ in Eq.~\eqref{w:blend} hands the production from amplification
to SA at the crossover: for $\chi\le1$ ($\sigma_P=0$) it returns the
amplification, leaving the $e^N$ growth unchanged, and once
$\sigma_P P_\mathrm{SA}$ overtakes it just past $\chi=1$ the source
reverts to standard SA with no laminar residue (a pressure-sensitive
residue left past $\chi=1$ would couple the transition front to the
outer pressure field). The destruction is not gated off but tied:
$\sigma_D$ tracks $\sigma_P$ with slope
$c_{b1}/(\kappa^2c_{w1})\approx0.249$ from the floor
$(1+c_{b2})/(\sigma c_{w1})\approx0.751$, the two summing to one by the
definition of $c_{w1}$. In standard SA the
working variable is exactly linear, $\tilde\nu=\kappa u_\tau y$, from
the wall through the log layer---the $f_{v2}$ construction holds
$\tilde S=\tilde\nu/(\kappa^2d^2)$, hence $r\equiv1$ and $f_w\equiv1$,
at every height, and the linear profile solves the full equation
exactly---and the tie is the weighting under which that solution
survives the gates, at every height and for any width $\tau$
(derivation in \S3.5; the wall-layer solve returns
$\tilde\nu=\kappa y^+$ with the intercept shift exactly zero, the
residual deviation being roundoff). In the laminar range ($\sigma_P=0$) the destruction floor is
inert at the disturbance's quadratic scale:
$D/P_{\mathrm{AI}}<10^{-3}$ up to the $\chi=1$ crossing at the airfoil
seed level (\S3.5). The limit $\sigma_t\to1$ recovers the unmodified
SA equation and its fully turbulent calibration.

Between the two limits the blend is an interpolation, and no physics is
claimed for it. The model does not attempt to represent the
transitional zone's internal dynamics---turbulent-spot formation,
growth, and merging, or the intermittency distribution they
produce---and nothing constrains what $P$ and $D$ should be
mid-handover. The target is deliberately narrower: the transition
location, and the macroscopic aerodynamics that follow from it. Because
the actively transitional region is usually short on the scale of the
geometry, the integrated quantities are insensitive to how the
interpolation crosses it, and $\sigma_t$ is asked only to cross
smoothly and monotonically; in flows where the transitional zone itself
occupies a macroscopically significant extent, this economy would need
revisiting.

\subsection{Cross-stream diffusion of the laminar disturbance}

The SA diffusion operator is retained in both regimes, but its
molecular coefficient is scaled by $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}<1$ in the
diffusion term of Eq.~\eqref{w:sa} only, leaving the mean-flow
viscosity, the SA production and destruction, and the definition
$\chi=\tilde\nu/\nu$ (formed with the true $\nu$) untouched. Only the
molecular coefficient is scaled: relative to it, the nonlinear
self-diffusion and the $c_{b2}$ gradient term are both smaller by a
factor $O(\chi)$ where $\chi\ll1$, so they remain as in baseline SA.
The reduction is needed because, while the disturbance is sub-$O(1)$,
the diffusion coefficient $(\nu+\tilde\nu)$ is dominated by the
molecular part $\nu$, which does not vanish as the working variable
does. In a thin, low-$Re_\theta$ laminar layer the amplifying band is
narrow and abuts the $\tilde\nu=0$ wall condition, so with the full
molecular coefficient the cross-stream smearing drains the disturbance
as fast as the gentle rate $a\,\omega$ grows it, and transition never
occurs. The drain is also uneven: relative to the growth it scales like
$1/Re_\theta$, hitting thin layers hardest and bending the envelope
away from the $e^N$ form. The value of $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}$ is
determined in \S3.2. The negative-$\tilde\nu$ safeguards carry over
with one consistency requirement: the negative-branch diffusion factor
$f_n$ must be formed with the scaled coefficient
$c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}\,\nu$---the negative-SA construction at a rescaled
molecular viscosity, which keeps the total diffusivity positive.

\subsection{Contact with the original SA calibrations}

The original model was built and calibrated in nested stages
(\S II.2--II.5 of Spalart \& Allmaras 1994). The first two---\S II.2,
\emph{Free shear flows}, where the molecular viscosity is excluded from
the equation outright and $c_{b1}$, $\sigma$, $c_{b2}$ are set by
mixing-layer and wake shear-stress levels, and \S II.3, \emph{Near-wall
region, high Reynolds number}, where $c_{w1}$ and the $f_w$ calibration
are set on the log layer and flat-plate skin friction with
$\chi=\kappa y^+\gg1$---are high-Reynolds-number calibrations,
formulated where $\chi$ is large or $\nu$ absent, and therefore
oblivious to the $O(1)$ range of $\chi$ this model repurposes: every
modification above is inert there, and those calibrations are
untouched. What the modifications do touch is \S II.4, \emph{Near-wall
region, finite Reynolds number}---the calibration that introduces
$\chi$ itself and sets the viscous functions $f_{v1}$, $f_{v2}$ through
the buffer layer, where $\chi$ passes through $O(1)$, the handover
gates run partially open, and the molecular diffusion is still
comparable to $\tilde\nu$---and \S II.5, \emph{Laminar region and trip
term}, which they replace outright: the trip devices are dropped, and
the laminar region becomes the model's active domain rather than a
region to suppress. The contact with \S II.4 is managed quantitatively
in \S3.5: the production gate vanishes for $\chi\le1$ and the
destruction floor is inert at the disturbance's quadratic scale,
$f_{v1}$ still switches on at the calibrated $\chi$ because $\chi$ is
formed with the true $\nu$, and the destruction tie ensures the
recovered log law
is standard SA's.

\subsection{Freestream seed}

A higher $Tu$ is a larger seed $\chi_\infty$ and thus fewer $e$-folds
to the handover: a case seeded with $\chi_\infty$ reaches the handover
where its envelope crosses $N_\mathrm{crit}=\ln(c_{v1}/\chi_\infty)$.
The map is Mack's $e^N$ critical-$N$-factor correlation, adopted
unchanged,
\begin{equation}
  N_\mathrm{crit}(Tu) = -8.43 - 2.4\ln(Tu_{\mathrm{frac}}), \qquad
  \chi_\infty = c_{v1}\,e^{-N_\mathrm{crit}} ,
  \label{w:mack}
\end{equation}
with $Tu_{\mathrm{frac}}$ the rms turbulence intensity as a fraction.
The correspondence is approximate: $e^N$ methods declare transition at a
sharp $N_\mathrm{crit}$, while the model's threshold is smeared over the
$\chi=1\to c_{v1}$ handover---about two $e$-folds of the envelope;
Fig.~7 quantifies the flat-plate consequence under a stated onset
convention.

\section{Calibration facts}

Five stages, mirroring the paper's Sec.~III: the eigenvalue piece
($a_{\max}$, taken at face value, + the envelope conditions and
instrument), the qualitative
constants, the attached-layer triple, the favorable-gradient add-ons,
and the decoupled turbulent-side pieces.

\subsection{Rate ceiling and envelope conditions}
Temporal Rayleigh problem on $U=\tanh y$:
$\omega_{i,\max}/\omega_{\mathrm{peak}}=0.1897$ at $\alpha\delta=0.44$;
$a_{\max}=0.19$. Instrument: disturbance transport marched on
Falkner--Skan similarity fields
(implicit, $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}/\sigma$ diffusion, $\hat\nu_\infty=1$
seed, wedge $\lambda_p$ in cliff and factor; no destruction, so the
envelope stays amplitude-free---the omission is quantified in \S3.5). Conditions: Blasius
envelope meets Drela--Giles at $N{=}1$ ($Re_\theta=338$) and $N{=}9$
($Re_\theta=1108$); separation-limit profile ($\beta=-0.1988$,
$H=3.98$) onset-to-transition mean rate $=$ Drela's.

\subsection{Qualitative constants (anchors re-solved per candidate)}
$c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}$: plateau $\le 1/8$ ($\pm2\%$ about the plateau
mean over $1/24$--$1/8$); $1/2$ sags mid-adverse $20\%$; $1$ collapses
$30$--$38\%$ with Blasius interior deviation doubled.
$c_A$: at $2$, separation anchor unreachable (mean saturates $5\%$
short, $s$ at the step bound; $Q=0.81$ at the separation-limit
$Re_\Omega$ peak, band mean $0.65$); at $8$, near-wall $Q$ doubles and
mid-adverse sags $29$--$36\%$.
$p$: at $2$, mid-adverse sags $\sim25\%$; at $8$, overshoots up to
$+19\%$.
Gated operating points at the canonical $c_A$ ($\arg\max Q\,S$,
$Re_\Omega\gg$ onset): Blasius $\Gamma=0.865$, $Q\,S=0.059$
($a=0.011$); separation limit $\Gamma=1.575$, $Q\,S=0.786$.

\subsection{Attached-layer triple}
Solution $(254,1.005,11)$, residuals $(+2.2\%,+0.2\%,0.04\%)$; Blasius
interior within $5\%$ of the correlation over $N\in[1,9]$. Solve
valley: $(g_c,s)$ slides $(1.005,11)\!\to\!(1.020,9.9)$ within $2.2\%$
residuals; mid-adverse interior moves $5$--$8\%$. $N{=}1$ level
sensitivity: floor $<3\%$, operational onset $\pm0.5\%$, $(g_c,s)$
$(0.99,12.9)\!\to\!(1.10,6.6)$ over $N_{\mathrm{low}}=0.5\!\to\!2$,
mid-adverse $+20\%/{-25\%}$.

\subsection{Favorable-gradient slopes}
\textbf{Onset delay.}
$K_\lambda = (d\ln Re_{\theta c}/d\beta)/(d\lambda_p^{\star}/d\beta)|_{\beta\to0}
\approx 12.3/2.0$; self-consistent fixed point $6.1$ (most-amplified
point $=$ rate maximum; the clearance-maximum definition gives $6.29$).
Raw kernel (no $\lambda_p$ terms): favorable $N{=}1$ crossings sit at
$Re_\theta=400$--$677$ over $\beta=0.05$--$1$, up to $10\times$ early of
Drela's implied station.
\textbf{Rate factor.} Cliff-only favorable family runs hot: mean $1.2$--$2.0\times$, late
$1.3$--$2.4\times$ over $\beta=0.05$--$1$. Fit
$\mathrm{mean}/\mathrm{Drela}|_{\beta=0.35}=1$ gives $K_r=5.47\to5.5$
(quadratic rational form; $e^{-K\lambda_p}$ and $1/(1+K\lambda_p)$ are
$C^0$ at the origin and give less flat families). Factored family:
mean $0.84$--$1.00\times$, late $0.92$--$1.17\times$; $N{=}1$ crossing
$1.19$--$1.27\times$ ($\beta\le0.35$), $1.06\times$ ($0.55$),
$0.85\times$ ($1$).

\subsection{Turbulent wall layer}
Constant-stress SA wall layer: $c_{\nu,\mathrm{ai}}=1/12$ alone (gates
held open) leaves the intercept unchanged exactly (the linear profile
has no curvature for the molecular term to act on; residual at
roundoff).
Destruction tie, derived from the linear-solution balance
($P=c_{b1}u_\tau^2$, $D=c_{w1}\kappa^2u_\tau^2$, self-diffusion
$(1+c_{b2})\kappa^2u_\tau^2/\sigma$; $r\equiv1$, $f_w\equiv1$ by the
$\tilde S$ identity): $\sigma_D=1-(c_{b1}/\kappa^2c_{w1})(1-\sigma_P)$;
$\tilde\nu=\kappa y^+$ exact at every height for any $\tau$ (solve
residual at roundoff; $\Delta B=0$ exactly). Laminar
back-reaction of the $0.751$ floor: $D/P_{\mathrm{AI}}<10^{-3}$ below
$\chi=1$ at $\chi_\infty=c_{v1}e^{-9}$; instrument anchors move $<1\%$
with the floor included, the $K_r$-point mean $-2.6\%$ (mostly past
$\chi=1$); CFD A/B: flat-plate onset $+0.8\%$ in $Re_\theta$ at
$Tu=0.04\%$, NLF a4 and Eppler a5 fronts/forces unchanged
(max$|\Delta C_f|\sim3\times10^{-5}$).

\section{Results}

Coupled RANS: Flow360, unstructured node-centered second-order finite
volume, Roe flux, $M=0.1$. Airfoil cases: $\chi_\infty=8.76\times10^{-4}$
($N_\mathrm{crit}=9$); two independently generated mesh families
(all-triangular cavity, all-quadrilateral O-grid), three refinement
levels each, four incidences per airfoil (24 cases per airfoil), plus a
five-point Reynolds sweep. Same constants everywhere.
